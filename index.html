How to Calculate Etsy Fees and Profit in 2026

If you're selling on Etsy, understanding your fees is the difference between a profitable shop and one that loses money without realizing it. This Etsy Fee Calculator was designed to give you instant clarity on every dollar — so you can price your products with confidence and grow a sustainable business.

💡 Quick fact: Many Etsy sellers underprice their products because they forget to account for all fees. Using an Etsy profit calculator before listing helps you avoid that costly mistake.

You input your product price, shipping charge, product cost, and shipping cost, and the calculator returns your total Etsy fees, net profit, and profit margin percentage in seconds.

How Etsy Fees Work in 2026

Etsy charges sellers through several different fee types. It's important to understand each one so you can accurately calculate Etsy profit online. Here's a breakdown of the main fees as of 2026:

1. Listing Fee — $0.20 Per Item

Every time you create or renew a listing on Etsy, you're charged a flat $0.20 listing fee. This fee applies whether or not the item sells. Listings automatically renew after 4 months or after a sale (for multi-quantity listings).

2. Transaction Fee — 6.5% of Sale + Shipping

This is Etsy's primary revenue source. The 6.5% transaction fee is calculated on the total amount the buyer pays, including the item price AND the shipping price you set. This is where many sellers get surprised — they forget that shipping is also fee-able.

3. Payment Processing Fee — 3% + $0.25

When buyers pay through Etsy Payments (which is mandatory in most countries), Etsy charges a payment processing fee of 3% + $0.25 per transaction in the US. This rate varies slightly by country but the structure is consistent.

4. Optional: Etsy Plus, Offsite Ads

If you subscribe to Etsy Plus ($10/month), you get additional credits and features. If Etsy runs an Offsite Ad for your product and it leads to a sale, you'll be charged an additional 12–15% advertising fee on top of other fees. Our calculator focuses on core mandatory fees.

How to Calculate Etsy Profit Step by Step

To manually calculate Etsy profit, follow this formula:

  • Revenue = Product Price + Shipping Price (charged to buyer)
  • Transaction Fee = Revenue × 6.5%
  • Listing Fee = $0.20 (flat)
  • Payment Processing Fee = Revenue × 3% + $0.25
  • Total Etsy Fees = Transaction Fee + Listing Fee + Payment Fee
  • Total Costs = Total Etsy Fees + Product Cost + Shipping Cost (you pay)
  • Net Profit = Revenue − Total Costs
  • Profit Margin % = (Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100

This looks simple, but doing it manually for every product variant is time-consuming and error-prone. That's why using our Etsy seller fees calculator saves you hours and helps you make smarter pricing decisions.

🧮 Example: Selling a candle for $22, shipping of $5. Product cost $6, shipping cost $4.
Transaction fee: ($22+$5) × 6.5% = $1.76. Listing: $0.20. Payment: ($27 × 3%) + $0.25 = $1.06. Total fees: $3.02. Total costs: $3.02 + $6 + $4 = $13.02. Net profit: $27 − $13.02 = $13.98 (51.8% margin).

Tips to Increase Profit on Etsy

1. Price for Profit, Not Just Competition

Don't race to the bottom. Use this Etsy fee calculator 2026 to understand your true break-even point, then price above it with healthy margin. A 30–50% profit margin is a sustainable target for most product categories.

2. Bundle Shipping Into Your Price

Offering "free shipping" can improve conversion rates AND simplify your fee calculation (since shipping is baked into the product price). Remember, Etsy's transaction fee still applies to the full buyer-facing price.

3. Reduce Product Costs Through Bulk Buying

One of the most effective ways to improve margin is to source materials in bulk. Even a 15–20% reduction in material cost can significantly improve your profit margin on each sale.

4. Maximize Your $0.20 Listing Value

Since the listing fee is flat regardless of price, higher-priced items have a proportionally lower listing fee impact. Consider consolidating similar products into single listings with variations rather than separate listings.

5. Track Every Sale with a Calculator

Use this free Etsy profit calculator for every new product you plan to list. It takes under a minute and ensures you never unknowingly sell at a loss.
